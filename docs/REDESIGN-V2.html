Audit & Redesign · v2 Blueprint · July 2026

De Waag 2.0 — from modules to a working process

A full audit of the current application, and a from-scratch redesign. The verdict in one sentence: the engine room is professional, the product is not — because it is organized around features instead of around your working day. Version 2 is a rebuild of everything you touch.

Part 1 — The audit

Finding 1 · No process spine The app is seven parallel pages. Professional investing is a pipeline: idea → triage → deep research → decision → live monitoring → post-mortem. Nothing in the current product moves work through stages, remembers where you left off, or tells you what's next. This is the root cause of "it doesn't help me" — it genuinely doesn't, it only responds.
Finding 2 · Passive by design Nothing runs without a click: no scheduled ingestion, no alerts firing while you're at work, no awareness of upcoming earnings or ex-dividend dates. A professional system's defining feature is that it works when you aren't there and greets you with "here's what changed and what needs you."
Finding 3 · Amateur onboarding & empty states The constitution is signed by editing a YAML file. The desk greets a new user with a lock. The portfolio pages are blank shells until trades exist. Real products carry you: a first-run wizard signs the constitution in the UI, builds a watchlist, schedules the jobs, and seeds the pipeline.
Finding 4 · Shallow data = shallow answers 45 tickers, ~4 years of statements, no sector classification, no peer comparison, no news, no calendar. A professional dossier must answer "versus whom?" and "what happens next week?" — the current company page cannot answer either.
Finding 5 · The teacher swallowed the tool Lesson references sit inside every card. Useful for learning; fatal for the feeling of professionalism. Pedagogy must become an optional layer (a "mentor mode" toggle, off by default) — present when wanted, invisible otherwise.
What survives the audit — stated honestly The invisible half is sound and matches institutional practice: point-in-time vault with quality gates, signal/risk/backtest engines with tested math, constitution-gated execution (the regulatory standard for pre-trade controls), journaled decisions. These become headless services under the new product. Throwing them away would be theater, not professionalism — the rebuild targets the experience, not the engine room.

Part 2 — The redesign: three surfaces, one pipeline

Version 2 abandons the module sidebar. The product has three surfaces, mapped to how a professional actually spends the day, plus a command palette (Ctrl+K) to jump anywhere.

Opens first, every time. One screen that answers: what happened, what needs me, what's coming. Composed automatically by the overnight job chain.

Surface 3 · LIBRARY — the knowledge base

  • Dossiers — deep company pages v2: candlestick+volume charts with peer/benchmark overlay, sector-aware metrics (banks get bank rulers — fixes the KBC false flags), peer table, news timeline, filings with agent summaries and citations, your notes and past pipeline history for that name.
  • Research — every thesis, memo, debate transcript, and post-mortem, searchable.
  • Lab — the backtester and experiment ledger (kept as-is; it earned its place by refuting momentum).
  • Journal analytics — your measured behavior: pass/buy ratios, thesis accuracy, hold times, the bias mirror.
  • System — data health, job runs, inference log, constitution (signed here, in the UI, with a proper flow — never again in YAML).

Part 3 — Build plan

R1 · foundations of the new product (~2 wks)

Scheduler + Calendar + Alerts + Onboarding

Nightly job chain registered in Windows Task Scheduler; earnings/ex-div calendar ingestion; alert engine (exit-level, 52w, earnings proximity, gate breaches); first-run wizard that signs the constitution in the UI. The app starts working while you sleep — the single biggest professionalism jump available.

R2 · the spine (~3 wks)

Pipeline + Today

PipelineStore, kanban board, stage automation (auto-dossier on triage, memo assembly on decision), the Today cockpit composed from jobs+alerts+tasks. The three-surface shell replaces the module sidebar; mentor mode becomes a toggle, default off.

R3 · depth (~3 wks)

Dossiers v2 + AgentService

Sector-aware metrics, peer tables, candlestick/volume/compare charts, news timeline; agents (session mode): dossier writer, bull/bear debate, filings summarizer, morning brief. The multimodal layer, alpha-miner style.

R4 · the loop closes (~2 wks)

Post-mortems + Journal analytics + IBKR

Close-triggered grading, bias dashboard, and the IBKR paper adapter the moment the account clears. From then on: live the process, harden what squeaks.

Decision needed to start: build R1. The v1 pages stay functional during the rebuild — surfaces replace them one at a time, no big-bang risk.
